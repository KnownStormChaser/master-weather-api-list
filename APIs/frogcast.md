### Frogcast

High-resolution, probabilistic, multi-model global forecast API aimed at professional decision-making (energy, agriculture, logistics, construction, insurance), blending 25+ NWP models with on-site observations and outputting 11 quantiles per variable alongside the mean — by point or by polygon, up to 15 days ahead.

- **Based in:** Le Bourget-du-Lac, Savoie, France (operated by QUOS Group; FROGCAST is a registered trademark of QUOS Group)
- **Access model:** Instant self-serve — sign up, get a token and 400 free requests with no credit card; all six subscription plans are self-serve. Larger-volume or custom needs go through sales contact
- **Pricing model:** Paid (one-time 400-request free trial, no ongoing monthly free tier — see limitations)
- **Coverage / data types:** point forecasts and polygon/area-aggregated forecasts (hourly, 1-hour time step, up to 15 days), real-time/current weather, historical weather data, and probabilistic output (11 quantiles p0–p100 plus the mean for every variable / confidence intervals); 30+ variables — air temperature (min/max/avg), precipitation (intensity & cumulative, with rain-vs-snow distinction), wind speed & direction (incl. 10 m and 100 m, gusts), relative humidity, atmospheric pressure, and solar & UV radiation. No severe-weather-alerts, radar/map-tile, or air-quality output product (visualization and alerting are left to the developer)
- **Free tier:** none ongoing — new accounts get a one-time 400 free API requests that don't expire (unless the account is inactive 365+ days, after which it's deleted); the trial is limited to a single location of your choice but otherwise exposes the full feature set (15-day forecast, probabilistic data, global coverage)
- **Paid tiers (EUR/month; annual billing ~10% off):** Starter €5 (400 req/mo); Build €10 (1,500); Develop €25 (4,000); Grow €55 (10,000); Expand €100 (30,000); Power €200 (75,000). Larger volumes / custom data modelling: custom / contact for pricing
- **Rate limits:** metered by monthly request quota per plan (400 → 75,000/mo); quotas reset each billing cycle and don't roll over, and upgrading mid-cycle resets the allowance rather than adding to it; over-quota requests return HTTP 429. No published per-minute limit. Forecasts update up to 8×/day; the site claims sub-100 ms responses and a 99.7% uptime SLA
- **Output formats:** JSON (the API docs state all responses are JSON; the marketing site also advertises CSV and NetCDF output)
- **Geographic strength:** global; strongest in Europe and especially France (home market), where dense on-site observations and high-resolution regional models apply, and anywhere the high-resolution WRF model (down to 1 km) is deployed for complex terrain, aided by 90 m topographic post-processing
- **Notable limitations:** the "free" allotment is a one-time 400-request trial rather than a renewing free tier — commercial use effectively requires a paid plan (this could be read as freemium given the non-expiring credits, but functionally there's no ongoing free tier); the trial is single-location; pricing is EUR-only and quota-based with no rollover and no carry-over on upgrade; no published per-minute rate limit; forecast + historical data only (no alerts, radar/satellite tiles, or air-quality endpoints); how polygon and multi-quantile queries count against the request quota isn't clearly published
- **Built on:** Frogcast's own multi-model blend — 25+ NWP models from national meteorological agencies, machine-learning-weighted per location and season against in-situ observations for bias correction, with high-resolution WRF (to 1 km) over complex terrain. Disclosed model inputs include Météo-France AROME / AROME-OM / ARPEGE, DWD ICON / ICON-EU / ICON-D2, UK Met Office UKV / UM Global, NOAA HRRR / RAP / NAM / GFS / GEFS, ECMWF IFS-HRES, Environment Canada GDPS, Copernicus CAMS, plus recently added ICON-2I and HARMONIE-NEA
- **Docs:** https://api.frogcast.com/rapidoc/
