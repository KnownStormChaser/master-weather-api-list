### Met Office (Weather DataHub)

The UK's national meteorological service, delivering its data commercially through the Weather DataHub API platform. Unlike most government services in this catalog, the Met Office monetizes access: each product family has a small free tier on top of self-serve paid subscriptions, with enterprise volumes by invoice.

- **Based in:** Exeter, Devon, UK
- **Access model:** Instant self-serve — register, subscribe to a product, and get an API key; free, trial, and paid subscription tiers are all self-serve (card payment). Enterprise volumes are invoice-based via the Data Provisioning team (`dpt@metoffice.gov.uk`); bespoke licensing terms via `licensing@metoffice.gov.uk`
- **Pricing model:** Freemium (per-product free tier plus self-serve paid subscriptions; enterprise by invoice)
- **Coverage / data types:** four separate product families — **Site-specific / Global Spot** (point forecasts: hourly, three-hourly, or daily, plus a blended probabilistic forecast); **Observations** (UK land surface observations); **Atmospheric** (raw NWP model data in GRIB2, including the global and UK-area models); and **Map images** (weather map image tiles). Each is subscribed to and keyed separately
- **Free tier:** per product — **Global Spot:** up to 360 calls/day (the blended probabilistic forecast instead offers a 30-day trial at up to 55 calls/day); **Land Observations:** up to 360 calls/day; **Atmospheric:** a Free plan of 1 GB/month plus a 30-day Trial of 60 GB; **Map images:** currently free at up to 1,000 images/day (limited-parameter initial release — temperature, precipitation rate, total cloud cover, MSLP)
- **Paid tiers:** *Global Spot* (per day, monthly, ex-VAT): 900 calls £8, 3,600 £30, 18,000 £140, 36,000 £240, 72,000 £420. *Blended probabilistic:* 550 calls £8 up to 1,200,000 calls £16,260. *Land Observations:* same ladder as Global Spot (900 £8 → 72,000 £420). *Atmospheric* (by monthly data volume): 10 GB £14, 25 GB £33, 50 GB £63, 100 GB £123, 150 GB £182, 250 GB £295, 400 GB £457, 600 GB £663, then invoice plans scaling to 10,000 GB+ (£7,577 base + £243 per extra 500 GB). All paid plans are self-serve and switchable; enterprise/large volumes by invoice
- **Rate limits:** call-based products are capped by a daily request limit that resets at 00:00 UTC; the Atmospheric (data-volume) products carry both a monthly GB allowance and a 130,000 requests/day limit per tier (also resetting 00:00 UTC)
- **Output formats:** JSON for site-specific/observations; GRIB2 for atmospheric model data; PNG for map images
- **Geographic strength:** the UK and the surrounding North Atlantic/European area (its high-resolution regional models); the global model provides worldwide point forecasts and gridded data, though regional resolution is highest over the UK
- **Notable limitations:** commercial by default — each of the four product families is a separate subscription with its own key and free-tier rules, so there's no single "everything" plan; the free tiers are genuinely small (360 calls/day on Global Spot and Observations; 1 GB/month on Atmospheric); pricing is GBP and excludes VAT (20% for UK customers); Atmospheric is billed by **data volume** (GB) rather than calls, so cost depends on how many parameters/runs you pull; the Map images service is free only during its limited initial release and will move to paid; Land Observations cover the UK only; use is governed by the Weather DataHub terms and conditions
- **Built on:** the Met Office's own Unified Model (UM) — its global and high-resolution UK-area (UKV) NWP configurations — plus the UK land observation network; the blended probabilistic site-specific forecast combines multiple model sources
