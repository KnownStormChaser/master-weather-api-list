### GribStream

Developer-focused weather-data API that exposes raw NWP model output, full forecast-run history, and reanalysis actuals from NOAA, ECMWF, Taiwan's CWA, and AI systems through one query interface — built for ML pipelines, backtesting, and analytics rather than curated point forecasts, with per-coordinate/grid queries, `asOf` model-run cutoffs, and CSV/JSON/NDJSON output.

- **Based in:** Not publicly disclosed — no registered entity, address, or governing-law clause appears on the site or in the terms/privacy policy (see Notable limitations)
- **Access model:** Instant self-serve — sign in with Google or GitHub, create an API token (free tier included); the six Pro plans are self-serve via Lemon Squeezy checkout. Larger-scale usage, custom plans, and heavy backfills go through sales contact (`info@gribstream.com`)
- **Pricing model:** Freemium (ongoing free plan with a daily credit limit; self-serve paid Pro tiers; larger/custom by contact)
- **Coverage / data types:** query layer over 67 datasets — live forecasts, forecast-run archives, and analysis/reanalysis actuals. NOAA families (NBM incl. Alaska/Hawaii/Puerto Rico/Guam/Oceanic, GFS/GDAS, HRRR incl. sub-hourly, RAP, NAM, RRFS, GEFS, CFS seasonal, RTMA/URMA analyses, CCPA precipitation analysis, SPC HREF severe-weather probabilities, AQM/NAQFC air quality, DAFS/GTGN aviation turbulence & icing, UV index, AIGFS/AIGEFS), ECMWF (IFS deterministic/ensemble/wave, AIFS, ERA5 reanalysis), Taiwan CWA WRF, Google GraphCast, NVIDIA FourCastNet, and DMI HARMONIE DINI. Deterministic, ensemble, probabilistic, wave, seasonal, analysis, reanalysis, and AI model types. Variables are selected by `(name, level, info)` grid selectors; supports derived expressions, condition filters, grid/area queries, ensemble members, and `asOf`/lead-time backtesting controls. No curated "current conditions" summary, severe-weather-alert text products, radar/satellite display imagery, or geocoding
- **Free tier:** ongoing free plan with a daily credit limit — exact free quota **not published** (shown only in the signed-in token dashboard)
- **Paid tiers (USD/month; daily credit limits):** Pro $9.90 (48,000 credits/day); Pro 2x $18.80 (96,000); Pro 4x $35.70 (192,000); Pro 8x $67.80 (384,000); Pro 16x $128.80 (768,000); Pro 32x $244.70 (1,536,000). Larger-scale, customized plans, and heavy backfills: custom / contact for pricing
- **Rate limits:** metered by a daily credit budget that resets at 00:00 UTC, where `credits = returned_valid_times × parameters × ceil(coordinates / 500)` (cache hits billed at 10%); separate short-interval burst throttling returns HTTP 429 with a `Retry-After`. No usage overages — once the daily limit is reached, requests are throttled/rejected until reset. No published fixed per-minute limit; requests over 10,000 coordinates are not cache-eligible
- **Output formats:** CSV (default), JSON, and NDJSON (chosen via the `Accept` header); gzip strongly recommended
- **Geographic strength:** global for the global models (GFS, ECMWF IFS/AIFS, ERA5, GEFS, CFS), but the deepest, highest-resolution, and most numerous datasets are US/CONUS (NBM, HRRR, RTMA/URMA, RAP, NAM, RRFS, SPC, AQM, DAFS); regional coverage for East Asia/western Pacific (CWA WRF) and NW Europe (DMI DINI)
- **Notable limitations:** returns raw model/grid fields addressed by `(name, level, info)` selectors, not a beginner-friendly conditions/alerts API — visualization and alerting are left to the developer, so there's a steeper learning curve than a plain "point → forecast JSON" service; archive depth varies widely by dataset and several are short rolling windows (e.g. many analysis/experimental feeds retain only the last 2–15 days, NBM archive begins 2020, HRRR 2014, ERA5 served from 2020, RTMA/URMA from 2018) — there's no single deep unified archive; the free-tier daily quota isn't published publicly; credit accounting (valid times × parameters × coordinate bundles, cache hits at 10%) can make cost estimation non-obvious; best-effort service provided "as is" with no SLA, uptime, or data-availability guarantee, and `asOf` reflects model-run time, not the moment data was live in the API (a look-ahead-bias caveat for backtesting); operator location and legal entity are not disclosed
- **Built on:** a query/extraction layer over publicly available NWP model data (much of it from AWS Open Data), surfaced directly rather than blended into a proprietary model — NOAA (NBM, GFS/GDAS, HRRR, RAP, NAM, RRFS, GEFS, CFS, RTMA/URMA, CCPA, SPC HREF, AQM/NAQFC, DAFS/GTGN, UVI, and NOAA EAGLE's AIGFS/AIGEFS), ECMWF (IFS, AIFS, ERA5), Taiwan CWA (WRF), Google (GraphCast), NVIDIA (FourCastNet), and DMI (HARMONIE DINI)
